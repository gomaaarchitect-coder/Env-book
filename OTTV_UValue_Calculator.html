OTTV Calculator

Use this tool to calculate the Overall Thermal Transmittance Value (OTTV) for any building envelope. You can work through the formula manually or use the live calculator.

What is OTTV? OTTV is the average rate of heat gain through the building envelope per unit of total envelope area (W/m²). It combines conductive and solar heat gain through walls, glazing, and the roof into a single performance metric.

The formula — three heat pathways

For each opaque or glazed surface, heat enters by three routes. You calculate each route separately, then sum everything and divide by total envelope area.

Pathway 1 — Wall conduction

Q_wall = A_wall × U_wall × ETD × colour_factor

A_wall = Total wall area × (1 − WWR) — the opaque part only
U_wall = Wall thermal transmittance (W/m²K)
ETD = Equivalent Temperature Difference — accounts for thermal mass, colour, and diurnal swing (°C)
Colour factor — Light: 1.0 · Medium: 1.25 · Dark: 1.5 (applied to ETD, walls only)

Pathway 2 — Glazing conduction

Q_glaze_cond = A_glaze × U_glaze × ΔT

A_glaze = Total wall area × WWR — the glazed part
U_glaze = Glazing U-value (W/m²K)
ΔT = Simple indoor/outdoor temperature difference (°C) — not ETD

Pathway 3 — Solar gain through glass

Q_glaze_sol = A_glaze × Isol × SHGC

Isol = Solar irradiance on the façade (W/m²) — depends on orientation
SHGC = Solar Heat Gain Coefficient of the glazing (0 to 1)

Final OTTV

Total Q = Σ (Q_wall + Q_glaze_cond + Q_glaze_sol)  [W]
OTTV = Total Q ÷ A_skin  [W/m² envelope]
Cooling load density = Total Q ÷ A_floor  [W/m² floor]

A_skin = sum of ALL wall areas + roof area (opaque + glazed combined). A_floor = total building floor area — this lets you compare heat load per usable square metre. The roof uses the same three pathways but with its own ETD, Isol, and U_roof values. Colour factor does NOT apply to the roof.

Worked example — North façade

Wall area: 40 m² · WWR: 10% · Colour: Light · ETD_N = 8°C · Isol_N = 450 W/m²

A_wall = 40 × (1 − 0.1) = 36 m²  |  A_glaze = 40 × 0.1 = 4 m²
Q_wall_cond = 36 × 2.5 × 8 × 1.0 = 720 W
Q_glaze_cond = 4 × 5.0 × 12 = 240 W
Q_glaze_sol = 4 × 450 × 0.9 = 1,620 W
Q_North = 2,580 W

Note: solar gain (1,620 W) is 63% of this façade's total — from only 4 m² of glass.
